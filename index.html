Esther Afua Ocloo

Entrepreneur

Timeline:

  • 1919- Born on the 18th April 1919 as Esther Afua Nkulenu in Peki Dzake, British Togoland, now Volta Region - Ghana.
  • 1936 - 1941 - Overcomes early childhood poverty during primary and secondadry school to win a scholarship to the prestigious Achimota School where she obtained her Cambridge School Certificate.
  • 1942 - 1948 - Starts a business supplying marmalade and orange juice to her former school; Achimota and the now defunct multi-battalion field force; The West African Frontier Force (WAFF).
  • 1949-1951 - Sponsored by Achimota School to travel to England for further studies. Obtained a cooking diploma from the Good Housekeeping Institute in London an a post graduate diploma in Food Preservation from Bristol University.
  • 1952 - Returns home to Ghana to get married and start a family with her husband: Stephen Ocloo.
  • Mid 1950s - Returns to England again to hone the skills for developing recipes for commercial canning On her return to Ghana, she formed a Manaufacturers' Association and helped organise the first "Made-in-Ghana" goods exhibition in 1958 to promote Ghana made goods and also to fight prejudice in Ghana against locallly produced goods.
  • 1959 - 1961 - Encouraged by then President of newly independent Ghana; Kwame Nkrumah, she was elected as the first President of what became the Federation of Ghana Industries.
  • 1964 - Becomes the first Ghanaian female to be appointed as Executive Chairperson of the National Food and Nutrition Board of Ghana. She also started to expand her business activities, venturing into the burgeoning tie and dye textile business.
  • 1970s - 1980s - Occupies a variety of senior roles at both national and international level in the economic empowerment of women:

    1. 1975 - Advisor to the First World Conference on Women in Mexico. Soon after this conference and noticing the difficulty most women faced with access to credit, she promoted the availablity of credit to women and pioneered the lending of small loans known as micro-credit. This small loan initiative was a game-changer as it allowed women who had no access to traditional bank business loans to acquire the necessary financial help they needed to start a small business.

    2. 1976 - 1986 - Appointed as an advisor to the Council of Women and Development

    3. 1978 - 1979 - A member of Ghana's Nationl Economic Advisory Committee

    4. 1979 - 1981 - A member of the Council of State in the Third Republic of Ghana

    5. 1979 - 1985 - A founding member and the first Chairperson of the Board of Directors of Women's World Banking

    6. 1990s onwards - She focussed on expanding her business, exporting her locally produced products to overseas. She also proposed alternative solutions to the problems of hunger, poverty and the distribution of wealth, championing the development of an indigenous economy based on agriculture. She set up a farm in Ghana specifically geared towards teaching women food cultivation.
  • 2002 - Died of Pneumonia in Accra, Ghana and was buried in her hometown of Peki Dzake - Volta Region.
